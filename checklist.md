# SOP Pre-Writing Question Checklist

Before drafting your Statement of Purpose, answer these questions for yourself.
Use them as notes; you will not copy all answers directly into the essay.

---

## 1. Your Interest in the Field

- [ ] When did your interest in this field first start? Can you recall a specific moment or period?
- [ ] How has your interest changed or deepened over time?
- [ ] What sub-areas or topics are you most excited about (e.g., bioinformatics, digital twins, AI for healthcare)?
- [ ] Which classes, projects, or labs most influenced your interest?
- [ ] Which papers, books, or articles have strongly shaped your thinking?
- [ ] Are there any key people (professors, mentors, supervisors) who influenced your interest? How?

---

## 2. Academic and Research Preparation

- [ ] What are the most important concepts you learned in your major?
- [ ] Which courses best prepared you for graduate-level work?
- [ ] What is the best paper, project, exam, or lab you’ve done in your field? Why?
- [ ] What research projects (if any) have you done? What were your responsibilities?
- [ ] What were the outcomes of your research (reports, code, presentations, publications)?
- [ ] What skills (technical, analytical, methodological) did you gain from these experiences?
- [ ] How does your academic record (GPA trends, strong semesters, challenging periods) reflect your growth?

---

## 3. Work, Internships, and Extracurriculars

- [ ] What internships, jobs, or volunteer roles are most relevant to your field?
- [ ] What did you actually do day-to-day? (Be specific.)
- [ ] What skills or perspectives did you gain that will help in grad school?
- [ ] Which extracurricular activities shaped your interests or character in a meaningful way?

---

## 4. Motivation for Graduate Study Now

- [ ] Why do you want to pursue graduate study instead of going straight into industry or staying in your current role?
- [ ] Why this degree level (Master’s vs PhD)?
- [ ] What do you hope to learn or gain that you cannot get elsewhere?
- [ ] What will you do between now and starting the program to prepare further (courses, research, work)?

---

## 5. Career Goals and Future Plans

- [ ] What are your specific short-term goals (0–5 years after graduation)?
- [ ] What are your longer-term goals (10+ years)? How do you imagine your career?
- [ ] How will this degree help you reach those goals?
- [ ] What kind of problems do you want to work on or solve?
- [ ] What kind of impact (scientific, social, industrial) do you hope to have?

---

## 6. Fit with the Program / University

For each program you apply to:

- [ ] Why did you choose this university and not another one?
- [ ] What do you know about this specific program’s strengths and focus areas?
- [ ] Which professors’ work aligns with your interests? What about their research attracts you?
- [ ] What courses, labs, centers, or projects at this school are especially exciting to you?
- [ ] What do you expect to get from this program (knowledge, skills, network, research environment)?
- [ ] What can you offer to this program in return (skills, experience, perspective, potential contributions)?

---

## 7. Personal Context and Unique Qualities

- [ ] What is unusual or unique about you compared to other applicants?
- [ ] Are there aspects of your family or personal background that genuinely shaped your interests or resilience?
- [ ] Have you faced any significant challenges or adversity? What did you learn, and how did you grow?
- [ ] What personal attributes (e.g., persistence, curiosity, independence, teamwork) will help you succeed in grad school?
- [ ] How do you handle stress and setbacks?
- [ ] What do you do outside academics for balance or creativity?

---

## 8. Addressing Weaknesses (Only If Necessary)

- [ ] Are there parts of your record (grades, gaps, test scores) that need brief context?
- [ ] Can you explain them honestly without blaming or whining?
- [ ] Can you show concrete improvement or lessons learned from these experiences?

---

## 9. Contribution to the Academic Community

- [ ] How will you contribute to the research environment (ideas, skills, collaboration)?
- [ ] How might you contribute to the broader community (teaching, mentoring, outreach, events)?
- [ ] Why would professors and peers be glad to have you in their group or cohort?

---

## 10. Quality Control Before Writing

- [ ] Do you clearly understand each program’s specific SOP prompt and word limit?
- [ ] Have you grouped your target schools into:
  - [ ] Identical questions
  - [ ] Similar questions
  - [ ] Unique questions
- [ ] Have you chosen 2–3 **core messages** you want every SOP to communicate?
- [ ] Are you prepared to customize each SOP for its program (especially the “why this school / professor / research area” part)?
